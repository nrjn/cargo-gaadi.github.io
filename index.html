What is a front-loader cargo bike?

A front-loader carries its cargo between the handlebar and the front wheel. The load sits low and central, so the bike balances and steers normally even fully loaded.

Front-loaders are used every day across Denmark, Germany and the Netherlands for school runs, deliveries and small businesses. In India, almost nobody has seen one yet.

Our design

This is Cargo Gaadi.

Our electric front-loader cargo bike. The frame is heavy-duty steel. A 20-inch front wheel keeps the cargo platform low. Easy to load, low centre of gravity. A 26-inch rear wheel matches what every Indian bicycle mechanic stocks. A mid-drive pedal-assist motor handles hills, headwinds and full loads. You still pedal. The motor multiplies what you put in.

Applications

What it carries.

A cargo bike replaces a scooter, an auto, or a small tempo for short and medium hauls. It works best when the trips are repetitive, the cargo is awkward and the route is narrow. For weekends, it's picnic gear, kids or camping kit for the hills.

Works best when

  • You move 20–100 kg of stuff
  • Your trips are under 20 km
  • You make the same trip many times a day
  • The route is narrow, restricted or congested
  • The cargo is awkward to carry on a scooter

Where it's useful

  • Farms & greenhousesMoving vegetable crates from rows to the packing shed.
  • Last-mile deliveryPackages through residential lanes that vans can't reach.
  • Heavy & awkward loadsWater cans, gas cylinders, building materials, paint buckets.
  • Factories & warehousesInternal logistics, parts between workstations, no diesel fumes indoors.
  • Small businessesDaily supply runs for the kirana, the bakery, the carpenter.
  • Mobile workshopsPlumber, electrician, gardener. Toolkit, ladder, supplies in one trip.
